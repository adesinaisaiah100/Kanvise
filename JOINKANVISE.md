## **01 — The Kanvise Story** 

## _**We didn't build a product. We built an answer.**_ 

In Nigeria, thousands of dedicated tutors show up every day — running JAMB classes on WhatsApp groups, chasing students for payments via DMs, manually sending attendance reports, cobbling together a 'school' from scattered tools that were never built for them. 

They are serious educators. But the infrastructure around them was not serious. 

Kanvise was born from the inside of that problem. Our founders didn't read about it in a report — they lived it. Running virtual tutorial centres, managing students manually, watching good tutors burn out not because they lacked skill, but because they lacked a system. 

The name Kanvise carries meaning. The logo is built from three elements: Structure. The letter K. An open book. That is exactly what we give every tutor who joins — structure, identity, and the tools to truly educate. 

We are not a marketplace. We are not a platform anyone can join. Kanvise is invite-only. Private. Professional. Built for tutors who are serious about running a real school — not just a group chat. 

We are building from Nigeria. But we are building for Africa. 

_**This is not another EdTech app. This is the infrastructure Africa's tutors have always deserved.**_

## **04 — The People We Serve** 

## **Primary: Virtual JAMB/WAEC Tutorial Centres** 

These are established virtual tutorial operations — often run by a lead tutor with multiple subject teachers. They have paying students, recurring classes, and real administrative pressure. They are the most urgent product-market fit. They feel the pain of managing everything manually the most. 

What they need: scheduling, attendance, performance reports, mock exams, material distribution, and a payment system. All in one place. 

## **Secondary: Solo Year-Round Tutors** 

Individual tutors who run classes on their own — usually across multiple subjects or a deep specialisation. They want to look more professional without hiring a team. Kanvise is their team. 

## **Tertiary: Skills & Vocational Tutors** 

Tutors outside traditional exam prep — coding, fashion, language learning, professional skills. They have the same operational problems but are often not yet thinking about LMS tools. A secondary audience for now.

## **Color Usage Rules** 

- Blue Rose (#2E2877) — primary brand color. Use for headlines, CTAs, nav, hero backgrounds, brand statements. 

- Ruddy Brown (#C26627) — secondary/accent. Use for highlights, hover states, accent borders, section markers, emphasis text.

## **07 — Experience Principles** 

Every touchpoint with Kanvise — a tweet, a landing page, a button, a notification — must make the user feel one of these things. These are not aspirations. They are requirements. 

## **1. Seen** 

Before we sell anything, we acknowledge the tutor's reality. We name their problem. We reflect their experience back to them with specificity. The first feeling should always be: 'they get it.' 

## **2. Credible** 

Kanvise must look and feel as professional as the schools we're helping tutors run. Nothing cheap. Nothing rushed. The visual quality signals that we take this as seriously as they do. 

## **3. Part of Something** 

We are building in public. Tutors who join early are pioneers, not beta testers. Every interaction should reinforce that they are part of a movement — not just downloading an app. 

## **4. In Control** 

The tutor is the school owner. Kanvise serves them. The app should never make them feel confused, dependent, or like they need a tutorial to navigate. Clarity is a design requirement. 

## **5. Proud** 

When a tutor uses Kanvise, they should feel proud to show it to their students. The app, the invite link, the school dashboard — it should feel like something worth sharing. 

## **Journey: From Discovery to Daily Use** 

|**Stage**|**What they should feel**|**How we achieve it**|
|---|---|---|
|**Social Media**|**Curiosity + Recognition**|Speak to their daily pain. Make<br>them stop scrolling. They should<br>think: 'this is talking to me.'|
|**Landing Page**|**Story + Belonging**|Tell the Kanvise story. Make them<br>feel they're being let into<br>something meaningful. Waitlist<br>signup should feel like joining a<br>movement.|

## **SECTION 2: THE PROBLEM (Story Section)** 

I attempted UTME three times before I got into Pharmacy. 

Three times. The kind of number that makes people start suggesting alternatives — "have you considered nursing?" The kind that makes you question whether you're chasing the right thing or just too stubborn to let go. 

When I missed OAU's cut-off for Pharmacy, I wailed. Not figuratively. I sat with that result and I wailed. That door had closed and I didn't know if another one was opening. 

UI's form was still open. I applied. But I didn't know anyone there, didn't know anything about the school, so I did what made sense — I found an online tutorial centre and signed up. 

I attended two classes. 

Just two. And then I stopped. And nobody noticed. No message, no check-in, no "hey, we haven't seen you in a while." Out of 100+ students enrolled, maybe a handful were actually showing up consistently. The rest of us? We just… quietly disappeared. 

I don't know how I scored 78/100 in the Post-UTME. Genuinely. But I know I was lucky. 

Because I had friends who weren't. Friends who fell through the crack — not because the tutorial failed them in some dramatic way, and not because they didn't try hard enough. They just slipped, silently, and no one caught them. 

That's the thing about virtual learning that nobody talks about enough. It's not the content that kills you. It's the silence. The invisibility. The way you can be enrolled and present on paper while being completely lost in practice, and the system never flags it. 

Kanvise exists because of those friends. Because "nobody noticed" is not a neutral outcome — it's a design failure. And I think we can build something better **.**

## **09 — Design Directives** 

For designers working on any Kanvise touchpoint — landing page, app UI, social assets, or print materials. 

## **Design Principles** 

- Structure first — layouts should feel organised and confident, never cluttered 

- Color with purpose — every color choice should map to a brand role (primary, accent, neutral) 

- White space is not wasted space — breathe room into every design 

- Mobile-first — most tutors will experience Kanvise on a phone 

- Poppins only — do not introduce any other typeface 

## **Landing Page Design Directives** 

- Hero background: Deep Blue Rose (#2E2877) with white headline text and Ruddy Brown CTA button 

- Section alternation: White and Rodeo Dust (#C2B59B at ~15% opacity) for a warm, rhythmic scroll 

- Section headers: Always Blue Rose, never black 

- Feature cards: White background, Blue Rose left border accent, Dark Grey body text 

- Founder section: Dark Grey or deep navy background — this section should feel personal and close 

- CTA sections: Blue Rose background, white text, Ruddy Brown button 

- Typography hierarchy: Headline (Bold 48–64px) → Subhead (Semibold → 

- 24–32px) Body (Regular 16–18px) 

- Avoid gradients unless extremely subtle — flat, purposeful color is the Kanvise aesthetic 

## **App UI Directives (Tutor Dashboard)** 

- Primary nav: Deep Blue Rose background with white icons and labels 

- Cards and panels: White with 8px border radius, subtle 1px border in Rodeo Dust or light grey 

- CTAs and primary actions: Blue Rose fill, white text 

- Secondary actions / links: Ruddy Brown text, no underline 

- Status indicators: Use Green for active/present, Amber for pending, Red for absent/overdue 

- Data tables: Alternate rows in white and Rodeo Dust at ~10% opacity for readability 

- Empty states: Warm and encouraging — never sterile. E.g., 'No classes scheduled yet. Set up your first one.' 

## **App UI Directives (Student View)** 

- Lighter overall — more white, more space 

- Blue Rose reserved for the school name/brand header to reinforce 'this is your school' 

- Ruddy Brown for progress indicators and upcoming deadlines 

- Class cards: clean, with time, subject and status clearly visible at a glance 

## **Social Media Assets** 

- All assets: 1080x1080 or 1080x1920 depending on format 

- Always include the Kanvise logo or wordmark 

- Use Blue Rose as the dominant background on most social content 

- Ruddy Brown for accent elements and highlights 

- Copy on social assets should be short, punchy, one idea per post 

- Never use stock photography of classrooms — we are abstract and structural in our visual language

## **Accessibility Requirements** 

- All text on Blue Rose (#2E2877) must be white — minimum contrast ratio 4.5:1 

- Never use Rodeo Dust (#C2B59B) as a text color on white — it fails contrast 

- All interactive elements must have :focus states using the primary color outline 

- Font size minimum: 14px for any visible text. Never go below. 

- All images must have descriptive alt text 

## **Do Not** 

- Do not hardcode any hex color values — always reference CSS variables 

- Do not use any font other than Poppins 

- Do not use pure black (#000000) — use Dark Grey (#3C3027) 

- Do not create new UI components without design sign-off from the brand lead
